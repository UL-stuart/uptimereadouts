# Rushstreet Score Notes Themes

Source: `rushstreet_airtable.csv`, column `Score Notes`.

These notes represent Rushstreet verbatim feedback on the drills themselves. This summary uses the 17 non-empty `Score Notes` entries in the Airtable export and keeps examples close to the original player/staff wording.

## Main Themes

### 1. The drills are consistently seen as valuable and enjoyable

The strongest theme is positive engagement with the drill format. Players repeatedly describe the sessions as interesting, valuable, enjoyable, or helpful, even when the drill is difficult.

- Armin, `Cart-astroph`, 1/4/2026: "the training is very valuable"
- Armin, `Details Matter`, 14/4/2026: "Amazing drill as always"
- Marija, `Profile Report`, 21/4/2026: the session was "always so fun"
- Marija, `PIR on Snipe Hunt`, 16/6/2026: "These session are really helpful"

### 2. Realism and difficulty are appreciated, not rejected

The feedback often frames difficulty as part of the value. Players like drills that are not straightforward, force them to adapt, or feel close to real incident pressure.

- Armin, `The Switch`, 17/2/2026: restarting the switch "failed whole site" and forced him to "think on other way"
- Armin, `Details Matter`, 14/4/2026: "It was not straight forward. Had to dig up a lot"
- Armin, `Snipe Hunt`, 19/5/2026: "difficult but definitely an interesting one"
- Marija, `Cart-astroph`, 7/4/2026: she chose the harder drill because "real incidents don't wait for good days"

### 3. Players value the feedback and reflection layer, especially PIR and Profile Report sessions

The non-drill review sessions are described as useful rather than administrative. Players respond positively to Profile Report and PIR sessions, especially when the feedback connects to their own data and incident habits.

- Armin, `Profile Report`, 3/2/2026: "He loves training and really like Profile Report"
- Marija, `PIR on Switch`, 10/3/2026: looking at her data from the slide deck was "interesting"
- Armin, `PIR on Details Matter`, 28/4/2026: "the session and training were really good"
- Armin, `PIR on Snipe Hunt`, 9/6/2026: he "found the feedback to be insitefull"

### 4. The drills are prompting concrete learning about process and incident handling

Several notes point to practical learning, not just general satisfaction. Players mention triage order, confidence during incidents, navigating distractions, and learning from the drill feedback.

- Marija, `The Switch`, 24/2/2026: she "learned a lot in regards to navigating distractions"
- Armin, `Details Matter`, 14/4/2026: "Need to change my process of triaging"
- Armin, `PIR on Details Matter`, 28/4/2026: the sessions help, with "confidence as the main benefit during incidents"
- Marija, `Snipe Hunt`, 19/5/2026: she was "very happy" to complete the drill

### 5. The content feels relevant to Rushstreet's own incident culture

The notes suggest the sessions connect with how Rushstreet already thinks about incidents. This is clearest in PIR feedback, where Marija relates the content to Rushstreet's own post-incident practices and blameless culture.

- Marija, `PIR on Switch`, 10/3/2026: the content "seems to match with what they do at rush street"
- Marija, `PIR on Switch`, 10/3/2026: they "try to promote a blameless culture"
- Armin, `The Chain,Grounding Workshop`, 20/1/2026: he had "similar events in his experience"

### 6. Early friction appears mostly as rustiness or time pressure, not rejection of the experience

The lower-scoring notes do not read as negative feedback about the drill concept. The clearest early friction is platform rustiness and not completing on time, while the same note still says the player enjoyed the session.

- Marija, `The Chain,Grounding Workshop`, 13/1/2026: she "felt a bit rusty on the platform"
- Marija, `The Chain,Grounding Workshop`, 13/1/2026: she "didn't complete the drill on time"
- Marija, `The Chain,Grounding Workshop`, 13/1/2026: when asked, "she said she did" enjoy the session

## Overall Readout

Rushstreet's score-note feedback is strongly positive. The most repeated signal is that the drills feel valuable, interesting, and helpful. Players particularly value scenarios that are realistic and challenging, and they respond well when the session includes reflective feedback about their own performance. The notes also show evidence of transfer: players are naming changes to triage process, confidence, distraction management, and incident-culture alignment rather than only saying the sessions were enjoyable.
