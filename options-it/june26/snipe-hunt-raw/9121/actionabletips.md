# Actionable Tips

1. **Own blocked approvals explicitly and earlier.** When the standard approvers are unavailable, make the decision statement personal and clear: "I am authorising this restart and accepting the risk." This will convert process management into visible incident ownership.

2. **Widen the change window once recent changes do not fit.** If same-day deploys do not explain the symptom, ask "what changed in the last week or two?" earlier. That habit will surface delayed couplings like cert rotations before a teammate has to find them.

3. **Ask one evidence-extending question after each teammate finding.** Instead of only accepting summaries, push one level deeper: "what else changed in that log after restart?" or "does the runbook mention this failure mode?" This builds your own model and improves F5/K4.
