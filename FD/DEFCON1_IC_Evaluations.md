# DEFCON 1 — Incident Commander Evaluations

Two runs of the **DEFCON 1** drill (ransomware-as-infra-failure, Grand Summer Sale) evaluated using the Incident Radar Analysis Prompt, grounded in resilience engineering (Woods, Hollnagel, Cook), naturalistic decision-making (Klein), and cognitive systems engineering (Allspaw).

Both transcripts anonymise the IC to `PLAYER`; the actual handle is recoverable from team @-mentions and is noted per session. The scenario is heavily scaffolded by NPCs — Maya (security) effectively drives the technical diagnosis and recovery, and uptimelabs auto-creates the incident record. Accordingly, the IC's job here is overwhelmingly **synthesis and broadcast**: capturing and naming the team's shared model, orchestrating the response, and brokering decisions with the business — not generating the technical findings personally.

**Key:** SM = Sensemaking, CO = Coordination, DM = Decision-Making, CM = Communication, AC = Adaptive Capacity, ILM = Incident Lifecycle Management

Both ICs ran the identical scenario and both performed above the prior DEFCON 1 baseline (Session 8749, 2.3). The shape of each run is similar because Maya and the uptimelabs scaffolding carry much of the technical arc; the differences are in IC craft.

- **Where 9300 leads:** structured, quantified communication; parallel delegation with ETAs; explicit business-approval gating; a clean, explicit closure with PIR capture. This is more mature *synthesis-and-broadcast* IC work.
- **Where 9314 leads:** the single most consequential decision of the drill. Shea **held off the script kill** (deferring to Maya, who confirmed it would have made things worse), whereas Joseph **authorised it** and the ransom doubled. On that one gamble, 9314 made the better bet.
- **Shared gap:** both leaned heavily on Maya and were slow to *name the frame shift* out loud. Neither IC drove the model; both relayed it. That is acceptable under the synthesis-and-broadcast philosophy, but both could have captured and broadcast the team's evolving picture more decisively at the inflection points (load → deliberate attack; contain → restore).
