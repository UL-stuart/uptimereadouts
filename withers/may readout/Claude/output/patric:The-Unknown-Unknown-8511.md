## Narrative Summary

PLAYER performed as a functional, competent incident commander who kept the response moving and avoided the most common failure modes — they did not disappear into technical troubleshooting, maintained communication with business stakeholders throughout, and made the right call to halt orders before the root cause was confirmed. Their dominant strength was pragmatic coordination: consistently directing named individuals, managing parallel threads, and acting decisively at key junctures despite incomplete information. The most significant gap was in shared sensemaking — PLAYER rarely externalised their mental model or narrated how their understanding was evolving, which meant the team was responding to directives rather than building a joint picture of the incident. Performance was broadly consistent across the drill, with a slight improvement in the resolution phase when PLAYER insisted on multi-party validation before bringing the site live. The single most impactful change for next time would be to narrate hypotheses aloud as they form ("my current theory is X, let's try to disprove it") and use explicit timeboxes on investigation tasks — both practices would sharpen the team's focus and surface the root cause faster.
