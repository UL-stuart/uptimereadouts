## Narrative Summary

PLAYER demonstrated a functionally complete incident command performance across a highly complex ransomware scenario, with particular strength in stakeholder communication and lifecycle management. External updates to business-comms were timely, honest about severity, and covered the key facts (global impact, 100% outage, $180K data loss, ransom confirmation), which is a genuine differentiator relative to ICs who neglect the business channel under pressure. The most significant gap was sensemaking visibility: PLAYER gathered data well but rarely externalised their working mental model to the technical team, leaving the shared situational picture to form organically rather than being shaped by the IC. The most consequential single error was ordering the kill-script at T+12 without first checking with Maya, which doubled the ransom demand — a vivid example of decisive action outpacing calibration. Performance improved as the incident matured: the structured isolation-then-restoration sequence, the explicit data-loss trade-off escalation to business, and the final independent 502 verification all showed good discipline in the back half. The single most impactful change for next time would be to pause before executing technically irreversible actions (script kills, rollbacks) and explicitly name the trade-off out loud — "if we do X, the risk is Y; Maya, are you comfortable?" — before acting.
