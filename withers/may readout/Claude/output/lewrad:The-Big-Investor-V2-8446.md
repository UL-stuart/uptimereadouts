## Narrative Summary

PLAYER's dominant strength was **structured external communication** — three proactive, cadenced updates in the business-comms channel with consistent format and clear next-update commitments, which kept the business stakeholder appropriately informed throughout a ~25-minute incident. The most significant gap was **internal sensemaking narration**: PLAYER asked good questions and delegated broadly, but rarely articulated a working hypothesis to the team or broadcast a current shared model — meaning responders had to infer what PLAYER was thinking rather than building on a jointly held picture. Performance was broadly steady across the incident, with no notable deterioration, though the most decisive moments (authorising the chaos-test stop, validating the fix) came somewhat reactively after hamed and tanya had already formed and proposed the solution. The single most impactful change for next time would be to **think out loud** — narrating the current hypothesis and explicitly revising it when new data arrives ("I thought this was a cart issue, but given the shipping logs, I now think we have a resource-exhaustion problem — let's focus there"). This habit alone would elevate coordination, sensemaking, and internal communication simultaneously.
