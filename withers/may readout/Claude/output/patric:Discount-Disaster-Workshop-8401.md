## Narrative Summary

Patric's dominant strength was proactive, calibrated external communication: the business-comms update at T+8 — containing status, working hypothesis, explicit uncertainty, and a 10-minute timeline commitment — was one of the earliest and most structurally complete external updates in this scenario, delivered without being prompted by the facilitator. He consistently delegated diagnostic work to the right named specialists rather than attempting hands-on investigation, and his bias toward action under uncertainty (stopping the load test before confirmation, requesting IP-level traffic suspension before a full forensic picture) kept the incident moving at pace.

His most significant gap was the absence of shared sensemaking and internal current-state broadcasting: at no point did Patric narrate the team's evolving understanding of the incident back to the group ("here is what we now believe is happening, here is what remains uncertain, here is our next move") — a practice that would have transformed him from a question-router into a genuine driver of joint situational awareness. Each frame revision — from capacity overload, to load-test contamination, to DoS attack — was absorbed and acted on individually rather than communicated as a revised shared picture.

Patric's performance was broadly stable across the incident, with no pronounced upward or downward arc; the post-mitigation phase showed some uncertainty about whether the incident was fully resolved ("Still see a high usage count," T+16, followed shortly by "Usage is dropping" at T+17), suggesting he would benefit from establishing clearer monitoring criteria for declaring resolution before beginning remediation. The single most impactful change for next time would be to institute a structured verbal model-update habit: at each major transition point (when the hypothesis changes, when a mitigation is applied, when evidence comes back), explicitly broadcast the current picture in one or two sentences to the whole channel — this practice alone would close the gap between his competent individual coordination and genuinely exemplary incident command.
